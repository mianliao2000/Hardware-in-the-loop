Description 
The XDPE1A2G5C digital dual loop 16 phase controller provides power for AI servers, workstation, and telecom 
applications that use AVSBUS. Voltage on either the first loop or the second loop can be provided by a multi-
phase buck converter with up to 16 synchronous-rectified channels.  Typical configurations of 16+0, 15+1, and 
up to 8+8 are supported. 
Command and monitoring functions are controlled through the PMBus and AVS interfaces which supports 
dynamic voltage identification with 1 mV/step, output range up to 3.1 V, offset and trim resolution of 625 uV 
and accuracy better than 0.5%.  
The XDPE1A2G5C controller utilizes digital technology to implement all control functions, providing the 
ultimate system solution in terms of flexibility and stability. Advanced control loop features, such as current 
mode control, variable frequency operation, Active Transient Response (ATR/FATR), fast DVID response, 
automatic phase shedding and Pulse Frequency Modulation (PFM) enable optimal response to a highly 
dynamic load with fast di/dt load transients across a wide range of load current.  
The XDPE1A2G5C controller supports multi-vendor pin compatible integrated power stages with trivalent PWM 
interfaces, fast response, integrated current sense, integrated temperature sense, and advanced fault 
detection capability, interface and signaling.  
In addition to supporting power stage fault protection features, the XDPE1A2G5C controller also includes a set 
of sophisticated over-voltage, under-voltage, over-temperature, over-current, and phase fault protections. 
These attributes provide a complete and advanced protection feature set for microprocessor and power 
systems. 
Infineon strongly recommends pairing Infineon’s power stages with our Digital XDP™ family of controllers to 
ensure correct interoperability.  Interoperability when pairing with other vendor power stages/ discrete power 
components cannot be guaranteed by Infineon and requires thorough evaluation and characterization by the 
power stage/ discrete power component vendor.

7 Theory of operation 
7.1 Startup configuration 
The XDPE1A2G5 is a digital power management controller with a firmware-based state-machine (See figure 
below). Operation is controlled by application specific firmware and configuration settings stored in ROM and 
NVM, then loaded into RAM and control registers during initialization. Configuration and firmware updates are 
supported, but the number of reprogramming cycles is limited. See Section 8.17 for more details on 
Configuration Management. 
 
Figure 5 State machine 
7.2 Power-up configuration 
Operating from a single +3.3 V (VDD) supply to the controller, an on-chip regulator generates an internal +1.2 V 
voltage. Controller operation is initialized by an internal threshold-based power-on reset circuit. During 
controller configuration, the contents of the NVM are downloaded into the control registers. During this period, 
the PWM outputs are held in high impedance (Hi-Z) state, allowing board pull-up or pull-down resistors to set 
the correct default levels for static input signals. To ensure no sensitive analog measurements such as XValent 
resistor decoding is executed prior to 3.3 V supply is settled, the controller provides a delay which extends the 
“Configuration/NVM Download” state between power-on reset and entering the Initialization state. This delay 
is used to ensure the internal analog circuitry settles before making any precision measurements that are 
performed in the next state.

7.3 Initialization 
During the Initialization state, the controller measures the external temperatures, input voltage, and executes 
the various calibration routines within the IC. Prior to exiting the Initialization state, the controller performs the 
external resistor pin set measurement that determines the PMBus (see Table 36) address. To properly set the 
device address, a resistor with at least a 1% tolerance must be connected from the ADDR pin to ground. 
7.4 Inactive state and TON_DELAY 
Upon completion of the Initialization process, the controller will enter the Inactive State. Before the system can 
be started, the controller will verify that the following conditions are satisfied: 
1. VDD > 2.95 V (start threshold) 
2. No shutdown faults are asserted that are defined in the programmed shutdown mask. 
3. VIN > VIN_ON threshold 
4. VAUX > VAUX_ON threshold (if pin is configured for Vaux sensing) 
5. Assertion of EN_Lx: It is recommended that EN_Lx be asserted only after VDD, VIN and power supplies for 
the power stages are ready.  EN_Lx is generally pulled up with an external resistor to the 3.3 V controller 
supply, but it is also compatible with a low voltage pull-up (e.g. 1.8 V) to allow for flexibility in system-level 
sequencing. 
6. Optionally, the XDPE1A2G5 controller may also be configured to wait for TSEN inputs to rise above 0.4 V (the 
valid temperature range) and/or the PWM voltage to rise above 1.0 V (indicating the trivalent bias resistor 
pull-up supply is valid) on all phases.  This allows the power stage driver to indicate its readiness via its 
TOUT or PWM I/O.  Alternatively, the XDPE1A2G5 controller may be configured to auto detect populated 
phases, where the PWM voltage indicates the presence of populated phases and the controller may start up 
with just the phases that were detected.  
Once the above startup conditions are satisfied, the controller will wait for a programmable period of time 
(TON_DELAY) before ramping up the output voltage.  
7.5 Soft-start 
Prior to entering the Active Regulation state, the controller performs a controlled, monotonic Soft-Start ramp 
of the voltage output. At the onset of Soft-Start, the controller will perform a pre-bias condition measurement 
of the output voltage. The controller will set the initial startup ramp voltage at the appropriate level so that it 
will not sink current from the pre-biased output. Soft-Start is performed by actively regulating the output 
voltage while digitally ramping up a reference voltage from the measured pre-biased voltage to its final target 
value.  
For the case when Vboot is a nonzero value, the output voltage will ramp to Vboot and assert the PG_Lx pin 
when the voltage ramps above the POWER_GOOD_ON threshold. The voltage will ramp at the rate set by the 
TON_RISE setting.  
 
7.6 Active regulation 
Each rail of the controller implements a variable switching frequency, digital PID current mode voltage error 
control loop and inner digital PI current control loop, input voltage feedforward and dual-edge PWM 
architecture. During operation, the output voltage is sensed differentially and digitized by a precision analog-
to-digital converter (ADC). The resultant digital error signal is then fed into a digital PID compensator. 
The locations of the poles and zeroes are determined by the digital loop coefficients Kp, Ki, Kd, Kfp1 and Kfp2, 
which are the PID (proportional, integral, and derivative) and low pass filter pole terms, respectively.

 The output of the PID compensator is scaled by 1 / Nph and becomes the target current for each phase.  The 
target current is compared against the measured sense current, and this error current feeds a PI compensator 
for each phase to generate a delta pulse width value that is combined with the feedforward duty cycle 
computation to determine the cycle width.   
 The cycle width for each phase current loop is converted to a PWM pulse using a digital dual-edge pulse width 
modulator, with programmable maximum duty cycle limit and phase current limit capability.  In addition to 
using this dual edge modulation scheme, the XDPTM controller also includes non-linear control mechanisms 
such as variable frequency and diode body braking high speed transient control, and frequency active transient 
response (FATR), to minimize voltage excursions during transient and repetitive transient events. 
7.7 Shutdown 
The shut-down state can be entered from either soft-start or active regulation states through user intervention 
(de-asserting EN_Lx) or through a detected fault including OTP, OCP, IUVP, OOVP and OUVP conditions. The 
controller’s two rails may be programmed to shut down together or independently in case of a fault condition.  
The XDPE1A2G5 controller supports two user selectable shutdown options in response to de-assertion of 
EN_Lx. The first option is a closed-loop shutdown where the controller ramps down the output voltage at a user 
defined slew rate. Once the output voltage has ramped to below 200 mV, the controller would switch off the 
output power stage FETs to avoid undershoot below GND due to residual inductor current. The second option 
is a hard-shutdown (Hi-Z) response, where the output stage power FETs are immediately switched off. In either 
case, the EN_Lx inputs are deglitched using the TOFF_DELAY setting to provide immunity to errant shutdowns 
due to noise on these pins. If immediate shut-down is required, TOFF_DELAY should be set to 0 msec. For cases 
where the shutdown is caused by a fault, the resultant shutdown response is always Hi-Z except for OOVP, in 
which case the low side FETs are turned on under this condition. 
7.8 PWM output configuration 
The switching frequencies for the rails are independently selectable and configurable by setting the 
appropriate register values for each rail output. PWM outputs are 3.3 V compatible trivalent signals which are 
set to high impedance during reset, configuration, initialization, and inactive states. PWM outputs can be 
assigned to any phase channel and to either loop. The multi-phase PWM output signals are flexible and can be 
configured to fire in any order that is required to take advantage of input and output ripple cancellation by 
interleaving adjacent phases, or to facilitate PCB layout.

8.2 Differential output voltage sense (VOSEN_Lx / VORTN_Lx)

8.3 Output phase current sense (ISENx) 
The phase current sense circuitry converts the differential signal of the remote current sense connected to the 
ISENx pins and the VD12 pin as a reference into a digital representation of the instantaneous phase current. 
The phase currents are used for current-mode control, computing total output current, current monitoring, 
telemetry, adaptive voltage positioning (AVP), phase current balance, phase error detection, positive and 
negative current limit, output over-current protection, and load adaptive response (auto phase shedding). The 
phase currents can be temperature compensated based on the internal die temperature of the controller. This 
insures that the integrated 1 kΩ resistors at each ISENx input are well compensated over the full temperature 
range. Current sense gain and offset parameters are trimmed at ATE which ensures a high degree of accuracy. 
Offset parameters are also continuously calibrated during controller operation to ensure good accuracy with 
varying operating conditions. 
Integrated current sense is a scheme using a power stage which incorporates a current or voltage output, 
IMON, proportional to the output current through the power stage high-side and low-side transistors. Some of 
Infineon’s integrated power stages vary their IMON gain as a function of output voltage (Duty Cycle), VDRV 
voltage, and current. The XDPE1A2G5 controller has implemented controls to compensate the varying IMON 
gain and produce exceptional phase current measuring accuracy. 
Figure 8 shows the recommended method to connect the integrated current sense signals between the 
XDPE1A2G5 controller and an integrated power stage. The controller supports an input signal range for 
integrated current sense of -200 mV to +650 mV (+550mV with the internal 1 kΩ) with 1750 µV resolution. This 
provides a phase current range of -40 A to +130 A (110 A with the internal 1 kΩ) with 0.35 A resolution.  The 
integrated power stage IMON pin output is a transconductance output of 5 µA/A.

8.4 Temperature sense (TSENx, Internal) 
XDPE1A2G5 controllers provide the capability to measure the temperature of up to two external temperature 
sensors. The temperature sensors are connected through the TSENx pins from the TMON output of an 
integrated power stage.  In the case of TDA21570 or equivalent power stages, this voltage is at a nominal value 
of 0.8 V @ 25 °C and a temperature slope of 8 mV/°C. The TMON (temperature output) pins of multiple power 
stages are OR-tied together so that the power stage with the hottest temperature (highest TMON voltage) will 
drive the signal line to the controller TSENx pin. 
Additionally, the power stage is able to provide catastrophic over temperature protection within the power 
stage itself to quickly trigger VR shutdown in case of temperatures exceeding the allowed junction limit. This is 
accomplished by the power stage driving its TMON/FLT to 3.3 V. The controller will respond to this when the 
TSENx voltage goes greater than 2.4 V typically. 
In additional to the two external temperature sense inputs, the XDPE1A2G5 incorporates an internal die 
temperature sensor (TSEN_INT). This is useful for local temperature monitoring, and for using the die 
temperature in device characterization and ATE trimming.

8.5 Auxiliary voltage sense (VAUX) 
The XDPE1A2G5 supports monitoring of an auxiliary supply rail (typically used to monitor Power Stage 
VDRV/VCC to control sequencing). The supply voltage on the VAUX pin is compared against a programmable 
threshold. Once the rising VAUX voltage crosses the turn-on threshold (Vaux ON) and ENx is asserted, all PWM 
outputs become active when there are no faults in the VR. The VAUX supply voltage is valid until it falls below 
the programmed turn-off level (Vaux UV).

8.6.2 Operation 
The interface operation consists of compl ementary functions in the controller and the target devices. The 
AVSBus Controller must initiate all data transfers and it must guarantee that AVS_ CData is held at a logic value 
‘1’ when the clock is not running. This must occur during initialization prior to any bus transfers, as well as 
during idling between frames. AVS_CData is not allowed to be at a logic value ‘0’ when the clock starts. 
The AVSBus Target listens for and may respo nd to controller commands but cannot initiate a transfer under 
any circumstances. In general , the target must guarantee that AVS_ TData is held at a logic value ‘1’ when the 
clock is not running. This must occur during initialization prior to any bus transf ers, between frames, as well as 
at any time during a transfer when the target is not sending data to the controller. An exception to this rule is 
that the AVSBus Target will set AVS_ TData to a logic value ‘0’ when it needs to alert the AVSBus Controller to 
start a frame so that it can send a “Status Response Frame” 
The XDPE1A2G5 implements mechanisms that allow recovery of the bus in case noise or some other artifact 
causes the bus to enter an incorrect state. One mechanism is target resynchronization. After receiving 34 clocks 
while the AVS_Cdata is held high, the device will resynchronize its bus interface and wait for the next start code. 
The second mechanism is a Bus Timeout. When the device determines that an ongoing transfer has been 
effectively aborted because the clock stopped running for a user programmable amount of time ( timeout), the 
device will reset the bus interface and again wait for the next start code.

The XDPE1A2G5 has a Mask Register that can mask any or all chosen warnings or faults listed above. Setting the

8.6.7 VOUT transition rate 
The XDPE1A2G5 supports dynamic VID changes by writing to the AVS ‘VOLTAGE’ command. The DVID slew rate 
is set by writing to the AVS ‘VOUT TRANSITION RATE’ command.  The slew rate can be set as low as 1 mV/µs up 
to 50 mV/µs.  The practical limit of the slew rate will be determined by the number of phases, per phase 
inductance, and the total output capacitance. 
During dynamic VID changes to a higher value, the tracking output over voltage protection (OOVP) is 
temporarily disabled. However, the fixed OOVP remains enabled.   
8.7 Phase Add/Drop: Autonomous 
The XDPE1A2G5 controller provides a selectable autonomous phase add/drop feature. By comparing the total 
measured current with a set of user programmable thresholds, the controller will autonomously add or drop 
phases. Phases are added or dropped one at a time based on the current level.  
Two types of user programmable parameters are provided for current based autonomous phase control: 
1. The threshold parameter determines the current level at which a phase is added. 
2. The hysteresis parameter, when subtracted from the threshold, determines the current level at which a 
phase is dropped. Independent threshold parameters are provided for each phase count transition. There is a 
single hysteresis parameter (6 A or 10 A) for all phase count transitions. 
In addition to the phase add method described above, the XDPE1A2G5 controller provides the following 
methods for immediate phase adding which helps to improve transient performance: 
1. When error voltage exceeds a programmable threshold (ATRH Entry Threshold).  
2. During DVID events.

8.8 Linear loop compensation (PID / Poles / PI) 
The XDPE1A2G5 uses digital voltage and current feedback loops to maintain a tightly regulated output voltage 
with inherent phase current balance. During operation, the output voltage is sensed differentially and digitized 
by a precision analog-to-digital converter (ADC). The digitized voltage is compared to a target voltage whose 
error is fed into a digital compensator that consists of PID parameters, Kp (proportional coefficient), Ki (integral 
coefficient), and Kd (derivative coefficient), as well as the low-pass filter pole locations, Kfp1 and Kfp2.  Various 
PID coefficient and Poles settings are available for optimizing loop response during soft-start, steady state, load 
transient, and power saving states. 
The output of each loop’s digital compensator is converted into a control current that is divided and fanned-
out equally to all active phases. The phase current is sensed and compared to a target emulation current. The 
error is digitized and is used to adjust the emulation current creating a synthesized phase current output. The 
control current going to the phases is compared to the synthesized phase current of each phase to create a 
phase current error that is feed into a digital compensator that consists of PI parameters. This is the inner 
phase current feedback loop whose output is converted into PWM pulses for each phase. The PI coefficients 
settings are available to optimize the inner loop response based on the output inductors used in the design.

In addition to the inherent phase current balance in current-mode control, the controller features High-speed 
Phase Balance control which is particularly valuable at load oscillation frequencies around the voltage 
regulator switching frequency. This control reduces “Beat Frequencies” that occur when the load oscillation 
frequency is equal to the switching frequency. 
The HSPB control monitors the peak current of each phase and compares it to the average current of all the 
active phases. When the peak current of one phase is greater than the current imbalance threshold for a 
programmable number of switching cycles, and the load oscillation frequency is greater than the HSPB Enable 
Threshold, the switching frequency will move to an alternate, and typically higher switching frequency. When 
switching at the alternate frequency and an imbalance occurs the switching frequency will revert back to the 
original frequency setting. It will also revert back when the load oscillation frequency goes below the HSPB 
Enable Threshold.

8.12.1 VDD under-voltage lockout (VDD UVLO) 
An analog comparator continuously monitors the +3.3 V (VDD) supply. When the supply falls below a fixed 
threshold of approximately 2.7 V, the VDD UVLO fault is asserted. A VDD UVLO assertion will trigger Hi-Z 
shutdown mode which immediately turns off both the high and low side MOSFETs of the power stage. 
 
8.12.2 Driver PWM Faults 
Driver PWM fault is designed to detect when a MOSFET driver/power stage is missing or not powered up. Prior 
to regulation, the controller will check the power stage’s PWM pin status. When the Infineon driver is powered 
up, it will drive the PWM voltage to a tri-state level of approximately 1.6 V. If the PWM pin is shorted or open, the 
controller will not send PWM signals out and it will report Driver PWM related faults as follows:   
• PWM shorted to GND or VDRV UVLO 
• PWM open 
 
8.12.3 Driver not ready – TMON low 
In addition to Driver PWM faults the controller also monitors the power stage’s TMON signal. Infineon’s 
integrated power stage TMON signal indicates whether the driver is ready and able to respond to PWM signals 
correctly. The controller will delay soft-start until the TMON voltage is greater than 0.28V (-40 C), ensuring that 
the driver has sufficient supply voltage to operate properly. 
8.12.4 Input voltage fault protection (IUVP/IOVP) 
The XDPE1A2G5 controller monitors the VIN (+12 V) supply with the VINSEN pin. The signal is low-pass filtered 
and compared against a set of user defined programmable thresholds to resolve VIN status conditions. Typical 
placement of the input under voltage (IUVP), the turn-on (VIN_ON), and input over voltage (IOVP) thresholds 
are shown in Figure 19.

8.12.7 Output over-current protection (OOCP) 
The controller calculates the total output current by summing the sampled phase currents from all active 
phases. The total current signal used for over-current detection is filtered through a programmable filter that is 
settable to four selections (63, 210, 380, 532 kHz). This provides the user the ability to tailor the required 
response time to both a fault shutdown and the assertion of a warning signal. Additional control of the 
response time is controlled via a fault and warning counter that is programmable from 0 to 31 1MHz clock 
cycles in 1 cycle increments. Typically, the fault limit, IOUT_OC_FAULT_LIMIT (46h), is set 120% higher than the 
expected maximum current, which is defined by the application/user.  The warning limit is defined by 
IOUT_OC_WARN_LIMIT (4Ah).

8.12.8 Per phase current protection (P2CL, NCL) 
P2CL 
Per phase pulse-by-pulse current limiting prevents inductor saturation by preventing the individual phase 
currents from exceeding a user-programmed threshold.  The threshold can be set up to 178 A in 0.35 A 
increments. The width limit of the PWM pulse is calculated based on the synthesized inductor current. Once the 
inductor current exceeds the maximum threshold limit, the PWM pulse is truncated to turn on the low-side FET 
for the rest of the PWM cycle. The P2CL fault monitors each phase independently.  
P2CL can be used to clamp the output current to insulate devices at the input side from delivering excessive 
power. The controller has been designed to ride thru peak current events.  The VR will assert the P2CL flag 
when the peak is 10A below the set value.  The VR will increase the FSW 1.625x as current continues to increase. 
From 500K up to 800K for the event depicted in Figure 23.   
This allows a system manager to receive a P2CL flag and throttle operation accordingly before VOUT droops to 
a dangerous level 
 
 
 
Figure 23 P2CL Response 
 
NCL 
The controller constantly monitors the inductor ripple current of each phase. Upon detection of negative 
current in the inductor exceeding a user-programmed threshold (up to -43.4 A in 1.4 A increments), the 
controller will set the PWM output of that phase to Hi-Z in order to reduce the negative current. The prevention 
of very high negative currents avoids potential damage to the high-side FET body diode during switching 
cycles.

8.12.9 Output over-power protection (OOPP) 
The controller monitors both the output current and the output voltage and uses this information to calculate 
the output power. An Output Over-power Warning will be issued when the power exceeds the user 
programmable threshold, POUT_OP_WARNING_LIMIT (6Ah).  Exceeding this limit does not cause a shutdown. 
Only the warning bit in VOUT_STATUS bit 0 and any configured warning outputs will be asserted. 
8.12.10 Over-temperature protection (OTP) 
Over Temperature Protection (OTP) is triggered when the operating temperature reported on TSEN1/2 exceeds 
the user-programmed shutdown threshold, OT_FAULT_LIMIT (4Fh).  
When selected for shutdown, an OTP fault will trigger a Hi-Z shutdown. Temperature protection is enabled after 
the Initialization state. 
8.12.11 Power stage fault detection 
In addition to directly interfacing with Infineon Power Stage to monitor temperature, the TSENx input is used to 
monitor a Power Stage fault condition. Any fault within the Power Stage is communicated to the controller by 
pulling its temperature sense output (TMON) to 3.3V. A 3.3V at the controller’s TSENx pin indic ates one of the 
following has occurred in the Power Stage:  
1) A power stage High-side FET Short (HSS) 
2) An over temperature fault (OT) 
3) A persistent overcurrent fault (OC) 
4) High-side driver voltage UV fault (Boot UVLO) 
Additionally, a Power Stage fault condition, VDRV UV fault, can be communicated to the controller by pulling its 
current sense output (IMON) to ground.  
The controller can shut down or continue operating and assert the SMB_ALERT# and/or FAULT1 pin upon 
receiving the power stage fault. 
The controller can be configured to accommodate future Power Stage Fault ID implementations. Consult with 
local Infineon representative for availability and enablement.

8.13 Active voltage positioning (AVP) 
The controller provides programmability of a linear DC load line slope via VOUT_DROOP (28h) register. The 
linear DC load line slope is programmable from 0 to 15.98 mΩ linear 11 mode with least significant bits (LSB) in 
steps ranging from 15.6 uΩ to 2.4 uΩ steps. The actual load line LSB is set in the vc_vavp_lsb_sel register.  The 
load line LSB can be set to 19.5 µΩ, 9.8 µΩ, 4.8 µΩ, or 2.4 µΩ.  
Additionally, a non-linear DC load line slope can be programmed. The slope of the non-linear load line can be 
broken into two additional segments. The load line slope of each segment is independently programmable 0 to 
15.98 mΩ in steps set by the vc_vavp_lsb_sel register and each segment slope will become effective when the 
load current exceeds the programmable current thresholds a shown in Figure 25.  
Linear DC load line is used to reduce peak overshoot voltages during load transient and helps reduce the 
required output capacitance. The non-linear load line shape shown in Figure 25 is typically used in applications 
whose transient load currents normally operate within the segment2 load line slope region. This allows for 
added overshoot margin during a load release typically seen in traditional DC load line applications, but it also 
limits both the maximum and minimum output voltage over the entire load range.

8.17 Configuration management 
The XDPE1A2G5 provides non-volatile memory (NVM) for storage of most of the programmable registers in the 
device. This includes all trim and configuration registers, and patch space for firmware updates. Thus, the 
controller can startup and operate autonomously after power-on reset of the device, without user or system 
intervention.

memory is split into 5 partitions as shown in Figure 35.  Partition 0 stores trim and user configuration space.  
Partition 1 stores the patch errata that is used to update the firmware stored in ROM.  Partition 2 can be used to 
store a cutomer specific patch.  Partition 4 stores up to ten black box data packets.  Partitioning and Patch 
Loading tools are available in the Infineon XDPTM Designer GUI and Gang Programmer Software.

8.18 Security 
8.18.1 Write Protect  
The WRITE_PROTECT (10h) command is used to control writing to the PMBus device. The intent of this 
command is to provide protection against accidental changes. The command settings of 0x20, 0x40, and 0x80 
are not intended to provide protection against deliberate or malicious changes to a device's configuration or 
operation. The command setting of 0x01 will provide an additional level of protection against deliberate or 
malicious changes because once set it cannot be changed until device reset. Available settings are shown in 
Table 40 below. STATUS_CML (7Eh) [6] "Invalid/Unsupported Data" is asserted when a protected command is 
written to.

8.18.2 Password Protect 
The XDPE controller provides a PMBus password protection feature, which prevents selected PMBus 
commands from being accidentally modified by unauthorized users. A password can be set up with the 
MFR_SETUP_PASSWORD (CAh) to protect PMBus commands selected in the MFR_SECURITY_BIT_MASK_LOW 
(D2h) and MFR_SECURITY_BIT_MASK_HIGH (D3h) commands. The length of the password is 4-bytes. A read of 
the MFR_SETUP_PASSWORD will return a hashed value of the set password. The password protection will 
remain disabled as long as there is no write to the MFR_SETUP_PASSWORD command and storage to OTP. The 
MFR_DISABLE_SECURITY_ONCE (CBh) command is used to disable the protection once the protection is 
enabled with a write to the MFR_SETUP_PASSWORD command. Entering the correct password at this 
command will unlock the protection. Reading MFR_DISABLE_SECURITY_ONCE command will provide the 
status of the security: 0 - Security Unlocked, 1 - Security Locked, 2 - Security Deadlocked (incorrect password 
enter 4x, reset to try again). STATUS_CML (7Eh) [6] "Invalid/Unsupported Data" is asserted when a protected 
command is written to.

11 Layout considerations 
In order to optimize voltage regulator performance, it is important to minimize the effects of printed circuit 
board (PCB) parasitic impedances on the digital controller and power stages. The following layout techniques 
highlight important practices which should be incorporated during design to optimize the heat-dissipating 
capabilities of the PCB. For additional layout guidelines and component placement tips, refer to AN002-
XDPE122xx. 
11.1 Component placement 
Within the allotted implementation area, orient the switching components first. The switching components are 
the most critical because they carry large amounts of energy and tend to generate high levels of noise. The 
power dissipation of those components should also be taken into consideration during PCB placement. Align 
the output inductors and power stages such that space between the components is minimized. It is 
recommended that the same placement is duplicated for all phases. 
The XDPE1A2G5C controller can be placed off to one side or centered relative to the individual phase switching 
components. Critical small signal components including the ISEN resistors, TSEN capacitors and voltage 
feedback RC filters should be placed near the controller. 
11.2 Routing of AVS signals 
AVS signal lines are very sensitive to noise sources and should be kept away from switching components. AVS 
signals need to be ground referenced and routed away from all noise sources such as power stages and switch 
nodes.
